The fried chicken demonstrates how cooking can change the texture and appearance of food through heat transfer and chemical reactions like the Maillard reaction.

\#\# üìö Core Science Concepts

1\. \*\*Mixtures\*\*: A combination of two or more substances where each retains its own properties.  
2\. \*\*States of Matter\*\*: Solids, liquids, and gases, and how they can change during cooking processes.  
3\. \*\*Heat Transfer\*\*: The movement of thermal energy from warmer objects to cooler objects.

\#\# üìñ Vocabulary

\- \*\*Mixture\*\*: A combination of two or more substances where each retains its own properties.  
\- \*\*Heterogeneous Mixture\*\*: A mixture where the components are not uniformly distributed.  
\- \*\*Heat Transfer\*\*: The movement of thermal energy from warmer objects to cooler objects.  
\- \*\*Maillard Reaction\*\*: A chemical reaction between amino acids and reducing sugars that gives browned food its distinctive flavor.
