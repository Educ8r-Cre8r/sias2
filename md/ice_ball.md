\#\# üî¨ Scientific Phenomena

This image represents the phenomenon of \*\*melting ice\*\* and the related concept of \*\*phase change\*\*. The ice is transitioning from a solid to a liquid state due to the absorption of thermal energy (heat) from its surroundings. This process occurs because the molecules in the ice gain enough energy to break free from their fixed positions, allowing them to flow as water. The visible droplets on the tray confirm that melting has occurred.

\#\# üìö Core Science Concepts

1\. \*\*Phase Change\*\*: The transition of matter from one state (solid, liquid, gas) to another due to changes in temperature or pressure.  
2\. \*\*Thermal Energy\*\*: The energy an object possesses due to the motion of its molecules; higher temperatures mean faster-moving molecules.  
3\. \*\*Melting\*\*: The process where a solid turns into a liquid when it absorbs heat.

\<pedagogical-tip\>  
Engage students with a hands-on activity: Provide small ice cubes and warm water in separate containers. Ask students to observe what happens over time. Introduce the concept of thermal energy by discussing why ice melts faster in warm water than in cool water. Use a thermometer to measure temperature changes and reinforce the idea that heat causes molecules to move faster.  
\</pedagogical-tip\>
