\#\# üî¨ Scientific Phenomena

This image demonstrates \*\*static electricity\*\* in action. When certain materials (like rubber or plastic) are rubbed against each other, they can gain an electric charge. In this case, the balloon likely gained a negative charge due to friction with another material (e.g., hair or clothing). The confetti, which is typically made of lightweight plastic, becomes polarized and attracts the charged balloon. This attraction causes the confetti to cling to the balloon's surface until the charges neutralize or are discharged.

\#\# üìö Core Concepts

1\. \*\*Static Electricity\*\*: An electric charge that builds up on the surface of objects, often due to friction.  
2\. \*\*Charge Separation\*\*: The transfer of electrons between materials when they rub against each other, creating positive and negative charges.  
3\. \*\*Electromagnetic Forces\*\*: The forces exerted by charged particles, which can attract or repel other charged objects.
