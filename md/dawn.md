\#\# üî¨ Scientific Phenomena

This image illustrates the phenomenon of \*\*condensation\*\* and \*\*atmospheric cooling\*\*. The fog or mist forming over the field is likely due to the temperature difference between the cooler air near the ground (especially in fields) and the warmer air above. As the sun sets, the ground cools rapidly, causing water vapor in the air to condense into tiny droplets, creating visible mist. This process is common in agricultural areas where moisture from irrigation or dew can linger.

\#\# üìñ Vocabulary

\- \*\*Condensation\*\*: The process where water vapor turns into liquid water.  
\- \*\*Water Vapor\*\*: Water in its gas form, invisible to the naked eye.  
\- \*\*Temperature Gradient\*\*: A difference in temperature between two areas or layers of air.  
\- \*\*Dew Point\*\*: The temperature at which air becomes saturated with moisture and condensation occurs.
