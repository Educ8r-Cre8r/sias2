\#\# üî¨ Scientific Phenomena

This image captures the life cycle of a cicada, specifically highlighting its metamorphosis from nymph to adult. Cicadas spend most of their lives underground as nymphs, feeding on sap from plant roots. When they emerge, they undergo a remarkable transformation, shedding their exoskeleton (a process called molting) to reveal their wings and adult form. The dew droplets in the image also illustrate surface tension, a property of water that allows it to form droplets rather than spreading out flat.

\#\# üìö Core Science Concepts

1\. \*\*Metamorphosis\*\*: The process of transformation from an immature form (nymph) to an adult, involving significant changes in structure and function.  
2\. \*\*Exoskeleton\*\*: A hard outer covering that provides support and protection for insects like cicadas.  
3\. \*\*Surface Tension\*\*: The elastic tendency of a liquid surface, which allows it to resist an external force.

\#\# üìñ Vocabulary

\- \*\*Metamorphosis:\*\* The process of transformation from an immature form to an adult.  
\- \*\*Exoskeleton:\*\* A hard outer covering that supports and protects an insect's body.  
\- \*\*Surface Tension:\*\* The property of water that allows it to form droplets.  
\- \*\*Nymph:\*\* The immature stage of an insect, such as a cicada, before it becomes an adult.  
\- \*\*Molting:\*\* The process of shedding an exoskeleton to grow or develop.
